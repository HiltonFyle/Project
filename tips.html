'' Fully vaccinated dosn't mean immune to covid-19''

As restrictions and mask mandates are dropped around the contry, people who are fully vaccinated
might be felling the return-to-normal fantasy. But Dr.Cardona warms that now still isn't the time to let
your guard down. While the vaccines are potent, there still a chance that you could be infected.

Dr. Cardona explains.

Fully vaccinated means that you completed a covid-19 vaccine series as recommeded for the best
protection against severe complications such as hospitalizations and/or death. No vaccine offer 100%
protection against illness, yet it does give you a better chance to fight off the infectious consequens of
being exposed to the SARS-Cov2 virus.''

Can fully vaccinated people still transmit the virus
to others, inculding other vaccinated people?

While it is still possible, Dr. Cardona says that the ability to transmit covid-19 may occur at a lower rate.
she adds that this could also be a ability for people who don't have a good immune response to vaccines.

''The elderly, those with immune or chronic health conditions or those with underiying health disorders
may not have the best protective responseto vaccines, such as the covid-19 vaccine. We are still collecting
and doing ongoing research about the vaccine response in these vulnerable populations''
